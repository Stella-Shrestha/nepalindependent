Entry Procedure to Nepal

Travelers can enter Nepal by air or by road. The entry process is handled through official immigration offices at international airport and selected land border points.

Before traveling, it is important to check passport validity, visa requirements, travel documents, and the latest immigration rules.

01

By Air

The major international airlines that operate scheduled services to Nepal include Royal Nepal Airlines Corporation, Aeroflot Russian Airlines, British Airways, Biman Bangladesh, China South West Airlines, Druk Air Bhutan, Gulf Air, Indian Airlines, Lufthansa, Pakistan International Airlines, Singapore Airlines, Thai Airways International, Transavia Airlines, and Qatar Airlines.

02

By Road

There are several entry points by land route. Among many entry points, Birganj, Belahia, and Kakarvitta are popular entry points at the India-Nepal border, while Kodari is an entry point at the Tibet-China-Nepal border.

03

Entry / Exit Points in Nepal

The entry and exit points for foreigners entering and departing from Nepal are managed through immigration offices. The immigration offices at such points are usually open 24 hours.

Entering and departing from other places except the official points is treated as a violation of the Immigration Act and the Regulation.

  • Immigration Office, Tribhuvan International Airport, Kathmandu
  • Immigration Office, Kakarvitta, Jhapa, Eastern Nepal
  • Immigration Office, Birganj, Parsa, Central Nepal
  • Immigration Office, Kodari, Sindhupalchowk, Northern Border
  • Immigration Office, Belahia, Bhairahawa, Rupandehi, Western Nepal
  • Immigration Office, Jamunaha, Nepalgunj, Banke, Mid Western Nepal
  • Immigration Office, Mohana, Dhangadhi, Kailali, Far Western Nepal
  • Immigration Office, Gaddachauki, Mahendranagar, Far Western Nepal
  • Immigration Office, Pokhara, not an entry point

Travel Note

Always check updated immigration rules and official travel requirements before entering Nepal. Rules may change depending on travel conditions and government regulations.
